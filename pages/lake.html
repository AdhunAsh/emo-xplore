Sorted places by traveller's interest

1.THUSHARAGIRI WATERFALLS

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Thusharagiri Waterfalls, located in Kozhikode district of Kerala, is a mesmerizing cascade nestled amidst lush greenery. Comprising of a series of waterfalls originating from the Western Ghats, it offers a breathtaking sight for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers alike. Visitors can trek through scenic trails, passing by gushing streams and dense forests, to reach the enchanting waterfalls. The picturesque surroundings and soothing sound of cascading water make Thusharagiri a perfect destination for relaxation and rejuvenation amidst nature's splendor.

2.NARANGATHODD

⭐⭐⭐⭐

Narangathode is a small village located in the Kozhikode district of Kerala, India. It's known for its scenic beauty, surrounded by lush greenery and rolling hills. The village offers a tranquil atmosphere and is a popular destination for nature lovers seeking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life

3.KAKKAYAM DAM

⭐⭐⭐⭐

Kakkayam Dam, situated in the Kozhikode district of Kerala, is a picturesque reservoir surrounded by dense forests and rolling hills. Built across the Kakkayam River, it not only serves as a vital water source but also offers a serene escape into nature. Visitors can indulge in activities like boating, fishing, and trekking in the scenic surroundings. The dam site is also popular for its scenic beauty, making it an ideal spot for picnics and leisurely walks. Kakkayam Dam is a hidden gem waiting to be explored, providing a tranquil retreat amidst the beauty of Kerala's landscape.

4.ARIPARA WATERFALLS

⭐⭐⭐⭐

Aripara Waterfalls, nestled in the lush greenery of Kozhikode district in Kerala, is a hidden gem awaiting discovery. Cascading gracefully amidst the tropical forest, this pristine waterfall offers a serene retreat for nature enthusiasts. Visitors can embark on a scenic trek through the verdant surroundings to reach the waterfall, where they can bask in the refreshing spray of water and immerse themselves in the tranquility of the natural setting. Aripara Waterfalls promises a rejuvenating experience amidst the beauty of Kerala's wilderness.

5.CANOLLY CANAL

⭐⭐⭐

The Canolly Canal is a historic waterway that dates back to the colonial era. Built in the 19th century during the British Raj, it served as an important transportation route for goods and people. Today, the Canolly Canal is a picturesque spot for leisurely boat rides, offering visitors a glimpse into the region's rich heritage and natural beauty. Surrounding the canal, you'll find lush greenery and quaint villages, providing a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Whether you're exploring by boat or simply strolling along its banks, the Canolly Canal offers a serene and scenic experience in Kozhikode.

7.KALLAI RIVER

⭐⭐⭐

The Kallai River is a historic waterway with significant cultural and economic importance. Known as the backbone of the region's timber industry during the colonial era, it played a crucial role in transporting teakwood from the nearby forests to the Kallai Port for export. Today, the Kallai River remains a vital lifeline for the local community, supporting activities such as fishing and transportation. Its scenic banks are dotted with coconut palms and small villages, offering picturesque views for visitors. Boating along the Kallai River provides a serene way to explore its natural beauty and learn about its rich heritage.
